A Little About Me

I am a technology enthusiast. Whether it's automating tasks on my desktop, installing custom ROMs on my smartphone, or learning a new JavaScript feature or framework, I find technology endlessly fascinating. Staying up to date with the latest ES6 ES2016 ES2017 ES.Next syntax feels like less of a chore and more of an opportunity to learn something new or gain a deeper understanding of an older topic.

Within the US school system a career in technology was never presented as something for me, and as such I never chose to pursue it within the realm of traditional education. After graduating from UC Santa Barbara with a degree in Biological Anthropology I still found myself with a desire to continue learning. Now, after 10 months and countless hours of self-teaching, web development has grown from a fun hobby to my career choice.

With such a large and constantly growing selection of technology, I believe it is possible to find a framework or language for almost any imaginable use case. Conversely, there is also the potential to become enamored with the latest trendy framework and to begin using it for every project, even if it falls outside of that framework's ideal use case. For example, this website was built using exclusively HTML and CSS. No JavaScript, no Bootstrap, no extra libraries (you can check out my source here!). While I would have loved to use React, that would have been overkill for this simple static website.

Like this website, each of the following projects was designed to show my competence in a particular technology.
